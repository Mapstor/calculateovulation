How to Choose a Fertility Tracker App

Before diving into specific apps, it helps to understand what features matter most for your situation. The "best" fertility app depends entirely on your personal preferences, tracking comfort level, and specific goals. According to a 2016 study in the Journal of the American Board of Family Medicine, the accuracy of fertility app predictions varies considerably, making your choice of app genuinely important.

Consider Your Tracking Style

Different apps cater to different tracking approaches:

  • Minimal tracking: If you just want to log period dates and get predictions, choose an app with a simple interface that doesn't overwhelm you with options.
  • Comprehensive tracking: If you want to track BBT, cervical mucus, OPKs, and symptoms, look for apps that support the fertility awareness method with charting capabilities.
  • Automated tracking: If you prefer technology to do the work, consider apps that pair with wearable devices to track temperature automatically while you sleep.

Free vs. Premium Features

Most fertility apps operate on a freemium model. Basic tracking is usually free, but advanced features like detailed cycle analysis, personalized insights, or device syncing often require a subscription. Consider whether the premium features will genuinely help your journey or if the free version is sufficient for your needs.

Algorithm Accuracy

Apps use different methods to predict ovulation:

  • Calendar-based: Uses average cycle length to estimate ovulation (least accurate)
  • Symptom-informed: Adjusts predictions based on symptoms you log (more accurate)
  • Physiological data: Uses BBT, hormone data, or biometric signals (most accurate)

The more data an app uses, the more personalized and accurate your predictions become. However, more data also means more effort on your part to track consistently. Research published in npj Digital Medicine analyzing over 600,000 menstrual cycles found that cycle variability is much wider than the textbook 28 days, reinforcing why personalized tracking matters.

Integration With Your Life

Think about how the app will fit into your routine:

  • Does it have reminders to log data at the right times?
  • Can it sync with health apps you already use (like Apple Health or Google Fit)?
  • Is the interface intuitive enough that you'll actually use it daily?
  • Does it offer a partner-sharing feature if that's important to you?

Algorithm-Based Prediction Apps

These popular apps use your logged data combined with proprietary algorithms to predict your fertile window. They're generally easy to use and don't require special equipment.

Flo Period and Ovulation Tracker

Flo is one of the most downloaded fertility apps worldwide, known for its user-friendly interface and AI-powered predictions. The app learns from your data over time, refining its predictions as it gathers more information about your unique patterns.

  • Best for: Beginners who want an easy-to-use, comprehensive app
  • Key features: Period and ovulation predictions, symptom tracking, daily health insights, expert articles, community forums
  • Tracking options: Period dates, symptoms, mood, sleep, water intake, physical activity, sexual activity, OPK results
  • Cost: Free with basic features; Premium subscription for advanced insights

Flo's strength lies in its simplicity and educational content. The app sends daily personalized articles based on where you are in your cycle. For those trying to conceive, the TTC mode adjusts the interface to focus on fertility-related features. Note that Flo faced scrutiny in 2021 from the FTC for sharing user health data with third parties, though the company has since implemented an "Anonymous Mode" and strengthened its privacy practices.

Clue Period Tracker

Clue takes a science-forward approach, partnering with research institutions and emphasizing evidence-based predictions. The app avoids the pink-and-flowers aesthetic of some trackers, opting for a clean, neutral design that appeals to users who want straightforward data without gendered visuals.

  • Best for: Users who appreciate science-backed features and a neutral interface
  • Key features: Cycle predictions, 30+ tracking categories, irregular cycle accommodation, cycle history analysis
  • Tracking options: Period, cervical fluid, pain, mood, energy, cravings, digestion, and many more
  • Cost: Free with comprehensive features; Clue Plus subscription for detailed analysis

Clue's algorithm adapts to irregular cycles better than many competitors, making it suitable for those whose periods don't follow a predictable pattern. The app also excels at correlating symptoms across your cycle to help you understand your body's patterns. Based in Berlin, Clue benefits from strong EU data protection laws, which is a notable advantage for privacy-conscious users.

Glow Fertility Tracker

Glow was designed specifically with conception in mind. The app offers detailed tracking and couples can share data, making it easier to coordinate timing efforts together.

  • Best for: Couples actively trying to conceive who want to track together
  • Key features: Partner syncing, community forums, ovulation predictions, medication and supplement tracking, appointment logging
  • Tracking options: Period, BBT, cervical mucus, OPKs, symptoms, intercourse, medications
  • Cost: Free basic features; Premium subscription for advanced analysis

Glow's community feature lets you connect with others on similar fertility journeys, which can provide valuable emotional support. The app also tracks medications and supplements, helpful for those taking prenatal vitamins or fertility medications.

Ovia Fertility Tracker

Ovia uses a proprietary algorithm developed in partnership with Harvard scientists. The app transitions through fertility, pregnancy, and parenting phases, making it a long-term companion. However, users should be aware that Ovia has drawn criticism for its data-sharing practices with employers through wellness programs.

  • Best for: Users who want a single app from TTC through parenthood
  • Key features: Science-backed predictions, nutrition guidance, health score tracking, pregnancy transition
  • Tracking options: Period, BBT, cervical mucus, OPKs, lifestyle factors, nutrition
  • Cost: Free (employer-sponsored model)

BBT-Focused Tracking Apps

These apps specialize in basal body temperature tracking, using the temperature shift pattern to confirm ovulation. They require more commitment but provide more physiological certainty about your cycle.

Why BBT Tracking Matters
  • BBT is the only non-invasive method that confirms ovulation has occurred (not just predicts it)
  • A sustained temperature rise of 0.2-0.5 degrees indicates progesterone production after ovulation
  • Combined with cervical mucus tracking, BBT provides a complete fertility picture
  • Research in Bioscience Reports confirms wearable temperature tracking correlates with menstrual cycle phases

Fertility Friend

Fertility Friend is considered the gold standard for BBT charting by many in the fertility awareness community. While the interface appears dated compared to newer apps, its charting capabilities and algorithm are exceptionally robust.

  • Best for: Serious trackers who want detailed BBT charting and analysis
  • Key features: Advanced BBT charting, coverline calculation, ovulation detection algorithm, chart overlay comparison, detailed statistics
  • Tracking options: BBT, cervical mucus, cervical position, OPKs, pregnancy tests, symptoms, intercourse timing
  • Cost: Robust free version; VIP membership for additional features

Fertility Friend's algorithm analyzes your chart to detect ovulation patterns and provides detailed interpretations. The app can detect ovulation using BBT alone, cervical signs alone, or a combination--and explains its reasoning. The Chart Gallery lets you compare your chart to others with similar patterns.

Kindara

Kindara modernizes BBT tracking with a beautiful interface while maintaining the rigor needed for fertility awareness. The app supports the Wink thermometer (their connected device) but works well without it too.

  • Best for: Users who want serious charting with a modern interface
  • Key features: BBT charting, fertility awareness education, community support, optional Wink thermometer integration
  • Tracking options: BBT, cervical fluid, cervical position, OPKs, symptoms
  • Cost: Free basic charting; Premium for advanced insights

Kindara's educational content helps users learn the fertility awareness method properly. The community forums provide peer support, and the app includes features to help you interpret your charts as you learn.

Premom Ovulation Tracker

Premom distinguishes itself with an AI-powered OPK photo reader that interprets your ovulation test strips. Simply photograph your test, and the app quantifies the line darkness and tracks LH progression across your cycle.

  • Best for: Users who rely heavily on ovulation predictor kits
  • Key features: OPK photo analysis, BBT charting, LH progression tracking, cycle comparison
  • Tracking options: OPK photos, BBT, period dates, cervical mucus, symptoms
  • Cost: Free with ads; Premium for advanced features

Premom is particularly useful if you use cheap LH test strips and want an objective way to compare line darkness day over day, rather than trying to eyeball whether today's test is darker than yesterday's.

OvuView

OvuView is an Android-only app that offers comprehensive symptothermal tracking with multiple interpretation methods. It's particularly popular among users who follow specific fertility awareness protocols.

  • Best for: Android users wanting detailed fertility awareness method support
  • Key features: Multiple symptothermal method support (Sensiplan, Marquette, etc.), detailed charting, backup and export
  • Tracking options: BBT, cervical mucus, cervical position, cycle symptoms
  • Cost: Free with ads; one-time purchase to remove ads

OvuView's strength is its support for multiple established fertility awareness protocols. If you've learned a specific method from a certified instructor, you can configure the app to follow those exact rules.

Device-Connected Apps

These apps pair with wearable devices or smart thermometers to collect data automatically, reducing the manual effort of tracking while potentially increasing accuracy.

Natural Cycles

Natural Cycles is FDA-cleared as a contraceptive app, indicating the rigor of its algorithm. The app uses BBT (measured with their thermometer or a compatible one) to identify fertile and non-fertile days with high accuracy. A 2017 study published in Contraception documented its typical-use Pearl Index.

  • Best for: Users who want clinically validated predictions and are committed to daily temperature taking
  • Key features: FDA-cleared algorithm, daily fertility status (green/red days), LH test integration, cycle statistics
  • Tracking options: BBT (required), LH tests, period dates
  • Cost: Subscription required (includes thermometer option)

Natural Cycles requires daily temperature measurement but provides clear green (not fertile) or red (fertile/potentially fertile) day designations. The algorithm becomes more personalized over time. For trying to conceive, the app indicates peak fertile days within the red day window.

Tempdrop

Tempdrop is a wearable armband that tracks temperature while you sleep, eliminating the need to wake at the same time each morning to take your temperature. The accompanying app charts your data automatically.

  • Best for: Those who struggle with consistent wake times or manual BBT tracking
  • Key features: Overnight continuous temperature monitoring, automatic charting, algorithm that filters out disturbances
  • Tracking options: Automatic temperature, manual cervical fluid entry, OPKs
  • Cost: One-time device purchase; app is free

Tempdrop's algorithm uses machine learning to identify your true basal temperature, filtering out disruptions from restless sleep, getting up at night, or inconsistent sleep schedules. The device also syncs with other charting apps like Fertility Friend for users who prefer a different interface.

Ava Fertility Tracker

The Ava bracelet tracks multiple physiological parameters overnight--not just temperature, but also pulse rate, breathing rate, and sleep quality. This multi-parameter approach aims to detect the fertile window earlier than BBT alone.

  • Best for: Tech-forward users who want comprehensive biometric tracking
  • Key features: Multi-parameter overnight tracking, real-time fertile window detection, pregnancy mode
  • Tracking options: Automatic (temperature, pulse, breathing, movement, sleep); manual additions available
  • Cost: Device purchase plus optional subscription for advanced features

Ava claims to detect five fertile days, while BBT typically only confirms ovulation after it occurs. Clinical studies support Ava's ability to detect the fertile window in real time for most users. The app provides detailed insights into your biometric patterns throughout your cycle.

Oura Ring Integration

The Oura Ring, primarily a sleep and fitness tracker, now includes cycle tracking features. While not a dedicated fertility app, it uses continuous temperature monitoring to predict periods and identify the fertile window.

  • Best for: Those already using Oura for sleep/fitness who want integrated cycle tracking
  • Key features: Automatic temperature tracking, period predictions, cycle insights within broader health data
  • Cost: Ring purchase plus monthly subscription

Oura's cycle tracking is newer and less specialized than dedicated fertility apps, but its advantage is integration with comprehensive health data. You can see how your sleep, activity, and recovery patterns correlate with your cycle phases.

Natural Family Planning Apps

These apps follow established natural family planning protocols, often developed in partnership with organizations that teach these methods. They're particularly suited for users who want to follow a specific, instructor-taught method.

Marquette Method Apps

The Marquette Method combines cervical mucus observation with Clearblue Fertility Monitor readings. Several apps support this protocol, including dedicated Marquette apps and general apps with Marquette-compatible settings.

  • Best for: Users following the Marquette Method with a trained instructor
  • Key features: Monitor reading integration, protocol-specific algorithms, instructor support compatibility
  • Cost: Varies by app; many are free or low-cost

The Marquette Method has been studied extensively at Marquette University, and apps following this protocol adhere to the established rules. Using these apps is most effective when combined with instruction from a certified Marquette teacher.

CycleProGo

CycleProGo supports multiple natural family planning methods and is designed to work with instruction from trained NFP educators. The app provides charting tools while the educational component comes from your instructor.

  • Best for: Users working with an NFP instructor who want a versatile charting tool
  • Key features: Multiple NFP method support, instructor portal, secure chart sharing
  • Cost: Subscription-based

Privacy Deep Dive: Who Sees Your Fertility Data?

Fertility apps collect some of the most intimate health data imaginable: menstrual dates, sexual activity, pregnancy attempts, symptoms, and biometric measurements. In the wake of the 2022 Dobbs decision in the United States, concerns about how this data could be accessed--by law enforcement, employers, insurers, or data brokers--have grown dramatically. The Electronic Frontier Foundation (EFF) has highlighted fertility apps as a particular area of concern for digital privacy.

What Data Do These Apps Actually Collect?

Beyond the fertility-specific data you deliberately enter, many apps also collect:

  • Device identifiers: Your phone model, operating system, unique advertising IDs
  • Location data: GPS coordinates, sometimes even when the app is not in use
  • Usage patterns: When you open the app, what features you use, how long sessions last
  • Third-party SDK data: Analytics tools embedded in the app that collect and transmit data independently
  • Contact information: Email, name, date of birth from account creation

Post-Dobbs Concerns

After the U.S. Supreme Court's Dobbs decision, experts raised alarms about fertility data being subpoenaed in states where abortion is restricted. Key concerns include:

  • Period tracking data could theoretically be used as evidence of pregnancy and subsequent termination
  • Apps storing data on U.S. servers may be compelled to hand over data in response to legal requests
  • Data brokers who purchase fertility app data could sell it to anyone, including law enforcement agencies
  • Employers using apps like Ovia through wellness programs gain insights into employees' reproductive plans

Privacy Red Flags

Be cautious of apps that:

  • Experienced data breaches without transparent disclosure

  • Review privacy settings and opt out of optional data sharing

Best Apps for Irregular Cycles

If your cycles vary significantly in length--shorter than 21 days, longer than 35 days, or unpredictable from month to month--standard calendar-based fertility apps will give you unreliable predictions. Conditions like PCOS, thyroid disorders, perimenopause, and stress can all cause irregular cycles. Here is which apps handle variability best.

Why Calendar Algorithms Fail With Irregular Cycles

Most period tracker apps predict ovulation by averaging your recent cycle lengths and applying a formula (typically "cycle length minus 14 days"). This approach assumes your luteal phase is a consistent 14 days and that your cycles are relatively predictable. For someone with cycles ranging from 25 to 45 days, these predictions will frequently miss the actual ovulation date by a week or more.

The solution is to use apps that rely on real-time physiological data rather than historical averages. Learning to recognize your own signs of ovulation is especially important when your cycles are unpredictable.

Top Picks for Irregular Cycles

  • Fertility Friend (Best Overall): Its multi-method algorithm detects ovulation from BBT shifts, cervical mucus patterns, or OPK results--independent of cycle length expectations. The app does not assume a fixed cycle and adjusts in real time.
  • Tempdrop (Best Wearable): Continuous temperature monitoring catches the thermal shift regardless of when it occurs in your cycle. Its ML algorithm handles inconsistent sleep schedules common in people with cycle irregularities.
  • Clue (Best Algorithm-Based): Among non-BBT apps, Clue handles irregular cycles most gracefully. Its algorithm accommodates wider variability and does not penalize you for skipped or delayed periods.
  • OvuView (Best for FAM Methods): Supports multiple symptothermal interpretation methods, letting you follow established protocols designed to handle cycle variability.

AI and Machine Learning in Cycle Prediction

Newer apps are using machine learning to improve predictions for irregular cycles:

  • Adaptive algorithms (Flo, Clue) continuously retrain on your data, weighting recent cycles more heavily
  • Pattern recognition identifies pre-ovulatory symptom clusters even when timing varies
  • Anomaly detection flags cycles that deviate from your personal norm, prompting you to seek medical advice

However, even the best AI cannot predict ovulation as accurately as real-time physiological tracking. If your cycles are irregular, investing in BBT tracking (manual or wearable) provides the most reliable ovulation confirmation.

How AI Algorithms Improve Fertility Predictions

Modern fertility apps increasingly rely on artificial intelligence and machine learning to move beyond simple calendar-based predictions. These algorithms analyze patterns in your logged data -- from BBT readings and cervical mucus observations to OPK results and symptom logs -- to build a personalized model of your unique cycle. But not all AI approaches are created equal, and understanding the differences can help you set realistic expectations for each app's predictions.

According to research published in npj Digital Medicine, the average menstrual cycle length varies far more than the textbook 28 days, with significant variation even within the same individual across cycles. This variability is precisely why static calendar methods fall short and why adaptive AI algorithms represent a meaningful improvement. The Healthline review of fertility apps notes that AI-powered apps can reduce the prediction window for ovulation from several days to as few as 1-2 days after sufficient learning cycles.

How Machine Learning Adapts to Your Cycle

The most sophisticated fertility algorithms work through a process similar to how your brain learns patterns, but with mathematical precision. During your first cycle with an app, predictions are based on population averages -- the app essentially guesses based on what is typical for women with similar cycle lengths. With each subsequent cycle of data, the algorithm identifies your individual patterns: your typical luteal phase length, how your ovulation signs correlate with actual ovulation timing, and whether your cycles follow consistent or variable patterns.

Apps like Natural Cycles use a Bayesian statistical framework, continuously updating the probability of fertility for each day as new data points arrive. Flo's algorithm incorporates neural networks trained on millions of anonymized cycles, while Clue uses a combination of statistical modeling and user-reported data to refine predictions. Ava's multi-parameter approach analyzes correlations between temperature, pulse rate, and breathing patterns that are difficult for a human to detect but that machine learning can leverage for earlier fertile window identification.

The key insight is that no algorithm can predict better than the data it receives. If you skip days of tracking, enter inaccurate data, or only log period dates without symptoms, even the most sophisticated AI will produce mediocre predictions. Consistent, honest data entry is the single most important factor in app accuracy -- more important than which specific app you choose. For the most reliable results, combine your app's AI predictions with real-time OPK testing during your predicted fertile window, and use BBT charting to confirm ovulation retroactively.

  • Multi-parameter wearable algorithms learn fastest due to continuous data
  • No app can compensate for inconsistent or missing data entry
